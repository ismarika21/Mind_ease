Key Insight: Post-Pandemic Surge

Google Trends shows a 270% increase in searches for "mental health apps" between 2019 and 2024. The sharpest increase occurred in 2020 during the COVID-19 pandemic, confirming that isolation, uncertainty, and social disruption significantly amplified Gen Z's mental health needs. This trend has not reversed — indicating sustained long-term market demand.

Research Conclusions

Key Market Insights

01

Digital Over Traditional

73% of Gen Z users prefer digital mental health support over traditional face-to-face therapy, citing convenience, privacy, and cost as primary reasons. Mobile-first platforms see 3× higher retention rates among 16–24 year olds.

02

Massive Untapped Market

The global mental health app market is valued at $6.2B and growing at 16% CAGR. Yet only 34% of Gen Z with mental health needs are currently using a dedicated app — representing a 66% untapped opportunity.

03

Stigma is Declining

Awareness of mental health has increased significantly in Gen Z: 65% openly discuss mental health online. This normalisation creates favorable conditions for adoption of stigma-free digital platforms.

"Gen Z is the first generation to grow up entirely online — and the first to demand that mental health support meet them in that digital space."
— McKinsey Health Institute, 2023 Gen Z Mental Health Report
